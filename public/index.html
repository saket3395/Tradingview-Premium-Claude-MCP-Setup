How to Use Signal Summary

Signal Summary reads the indicators already on your active TradingView chart (EMA stack, RSI, Balance of Power, VWAP) and distils them into an at-a-glance intraday read. It does not place trades and never invents data — anything missing from the chart shows as n/a.

How to combine the metrics: start with Market Bias + Trend (direction), confirm with Signal Strength + Confidence (is the evidence lopsided or conflicted?), sanity-check Volume Confirmation and Risk Level, then read Entry Readiness as the verdict: Ready = aligned high-conviction setup, Wait = directional but not yet clean, Avoid = the Avoid Trade reason tells you why (conflict, chop, or chase risk).

Metric guide: Market/Long-vs-Short Bias — net directional vote of the on-chart signals · Signal Strength — how one-sided that vote is (0–100) · Confidence — High/Good/Fair from strength · Trend — EMA-stack structure (Up/Down/Sideways) · Volume Confirmation — rVol read if a volume study is present · Risk Level — elevated on RSI extremes / large moves · Best Setup — the play that fits the current read · Trade Quality — A/B/C grade · Entry Readiness — Ready / Wait / Avoid.

This is a decision aid, not a signal to act blindly — always confirm against price at your own levels and the TPO scanner’s fixed entry/SL/target plan before trading.

        How it works

        Stage 1 — broad scan (automatic). Every cycle the server queries TradingView's public scanner for the entire NSE universe (~2,900 primary stocks) and scores each one. NSE streams real-time anonymously. No chart is touched — the scan runs server-side.

        Conviction score (0–100) blends native Market-Profile concepts from real fields: Opening Location, Value Acceptance/Rejection, IB / Range-Extension trend-day proxy, Relative Volume, NIFTY alignment, and R:R geometry. Only setups scoring ≥70 with R:R ≥1.5 and rVol ≥1.2 are shown — thresholds in config/markets.json → tpo.india.

        Stage 2 — confirm (on demand). Confirm loads the symbol on your active TradingView chart to read live OHLC and any Volume/Market-Profile study, and fetches the stock's real NSE upper/lower circuit from Upstox to validate the targets/SL.

        Honest caveats: the scanner's “VWAP” field is the pivot (H+L+C)/3 (labeled Pivot, used for scoring only — never for entry). Full letter-by-letter TPO needs intraday time-at-price and isn’t computable for the whole universe from day-level data — Stage 1 is profile-informed; Stage 2 gives true on-chart levels.

        How to Trade This Signal

        Stable entry, by design. Each entry is anchored to a fixed session level (today’s Open or prior-day close) and frozen the moment the setup first qualifies — it will not drift as the LTP ticks. You get one level you can actually rest an order around all day. The State column tells you what to do with it.

        When to enter. Act when State is VALID — price is in the Entry Zone (a breakout-retest / value-acceptance into the anchor). Prefer a confirmed hold (a candle close holding the level) over the first touch. ARMED = the plan is set but price hasn’t reached the zone — wait and set an alert.

        When NOT to enter. EXTENDED — price already ran >0.5R past entry: do not chase; wait for a pullback into the zone. INVALID — the stop level was reached, setup is dead for the day. EXPIRED — market closed, or under the late-session cutoff (no new intraday entries). TARGET — Target 1 already hit; only manage a runner.

        Entry validity & expiry. The plan is valid for the current session only and frozen per symbol per day. New entries are blocked in the final noNewEntryBeforeCloseMin minutes (default 45) and everything expires at the close.

        SL placement. Fixed at the tighter of the day extreme or a 1-ATR stop beyond the anchor, then clamped to the NSE lower circuit (Stage-1 uses an assumed band; Confirm swaps in the real circuit from Upstox). Never widen the stop; if hit, the setup is invalid.

        Target logic. Two range-extension objectives projected beyond the session extreme, capped at the NSE upper circuit (targets can never sit past the circuit; R:R is recomputed if capped).

        Risk management. Risk a fixed % of capital per trade (size = risk-₹ ÷ |entry−SL|). Book or trail at Target 1; move to breakeven once price clears ~1R. One clean A/High setup beats five marginal ones. Respect the checklist on the Dashboard before committing.

        Example workflow: spot a VALID LONG → open Dashboard, run the India checklist (index aligned, above VWAP, rVol>1.5) → click Confirm to load the chart and pull the real circuit → enter in the Entry Zone on a hold → hard SL at the shown level → book/trail at Target 1 → square off before 15:20 IST. Analysis only — no orders are placed for you.

        How Testing works (no mock data)

        Forward-test journal. Every plan the TPO scanners freeze is recorded automatically. A plan only counts as a trade once price actually traded into the entry zone (state reached VALID) — what a real trader could have filled. Plans that ran to target or stop without filling are tracked separately as missed (fill-rate), never in PF/WR.

        Outcomes: WIN = Target-1 hit (realized R = planned R:R) · LOSS = stop hit (−1R) · SCRATCH = session expired, marked at the last price. Stats need n ≥ 20 closed trades before gates show pass/fail — until then they read insufficient sample, honestly.
